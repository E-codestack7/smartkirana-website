Analysis of Digital Payment Adoption in Kirana Stores

Abstract

Background: Digital payments have grown rapidly in India, but adoption among Kirana stores remains uneven.

Objective: This study examines factors affecting digital payment adoption and its impact on small retail businesses.

Methods: A survey of 500 Kirana stores in tier 2 and tier 3 cities was conducted using questionnaires and interviews.

Results: About 62% of stores adopted digital payments, mainly UPI. Ease of use, customer demand, and incentives were key drivers (p < 0.001), while fees and security concerns were major barriers.

Conclusion: Focused awareness and training programs can significantly improve digital payment adoption among Kirana stores.
